Site Plan: At‑Home Piano Lessons

A simple plan for an at‑home piano lessons website.

Site Name

At‑Home Piano Lessons

I chose this name because it immediately tells visitors what the service is: piano lessons taught in the student’s home. It’s straightforward, easy to remember, and feels like something a parent or beginner would quickly understand when searching online. This project is also based on real lessons my wife teaches, so the name fits the actual service.

Site Purpose

The main purpose of this website is to help potential students (and parents) understand what in‑home piano lessons involve and what they can expect. The site will explain lesson options for different skill levels, share basic practice guidance to support progress, and provide an easy way to contact the instructor for questions or scheduling.

Overall, I want the site to feel welcoming and helpful—especially for beginners who might feel unsure about where to start.

Scenarios (User Questions)

These are examples of questions I expect visitors to have when they land on the site:

  • “I’ve never played piano before—what lesson level should I start with?”
  • “How do I contact the instructor and get started with in‑home lessons?”

Color Scheme

I chose a simple color palette that feels clean, calm, and easy to read. The blue works well for important actions like buttons and links, and the light gray helps sections feel organized without being distracting.

Brand Blue
#1e88e5
Used for buttons, links, headings, and accents

Soft Surface Gray
#f6f8fb
Used for backgrounds, section panels, and cards

Typography

The site will use a clean system font stack so it loads fast and stays readable across devices. This also keeps the design consistent and simple.

  • Headings: system-ui, Segoe UI, Roboto, sans-serif
  • Body Text: system-ui, Segoe UI, Roboto, sans-serif
